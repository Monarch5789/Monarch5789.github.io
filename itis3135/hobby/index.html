Who

Hello! My name is Aidan Perkins. I'm originally from Hillsborough New Jersey, and have a background in performing arts and computer science. I started with both at a fairly young age in middle school, and both continue to be passions to this day. Although I have decided to make my career in computer science, I still very much enjoy music as a hobby.

What

"Music" alone is a very broad description of my hobby. It would be more precise to say, I play both drums and the guitar. Music has been proven to be an excellent creative outlet as well as a good means of teaching discipline, memory, and more. When I first started playing, none of this was important to me, however, because I simply found it fun.

When

I truly started with music in fifth grade, when I joined the intermediate school band on a whim. I first joined playing trumpet, but quickly realized it was not for me. At the suggestion of a friend, I tried switching to drums, and have never looked back. Several years later (after graduating high school) I decided I wanted to start learning the guitar, so I bought one and started the slow process of teaching myself.

When playing or practicing, it is important to keep two things in mind. First, remember to take breaks. If you continually push yourself it can become very easy to burn out and lose interest. Additionally, be mindful of your neighbors or roommates, as music by definition creates noise, which can be disruptive.

Where

Music can be done anywhere in the world (and even outside of the Earth in the case of the Voyager probes). It's taken me to places I never expected to go. If you chose to take up music, it is something you can do pretty much wherever you are. Whether its performing on a stage or just in your bedroom, it doesn't matter.

How

Learning how to play music is a topic that has been discussed and evolved on for centuries. For some, it is possible to teach yourself how to play an instrument. For others, guided practice is necessary. Many businesses and individuals provide these services. Some lessons can be video based online, or in person with a 1-on-1 structure.


My experience began with lessons in a classroom, and then evolved into one-on-one sessions with an instructor. When I started learning guitar, I decided to try teaching myself. Overall it has proven to be an exciting challenge.

Why

For me personally, one of the biggest motivations for continuing in music has been the social aspect. During the time I spent in marching band, I made friends that I still talk to, and made bonds that will last a lifetime. Additionally, even today, I still connect with people over my passion for music.

Outside of that, music is rewarding in and of itself. Being able to play through a challenging piece, or even being able to write your own music is a sense of accomplishment that is hard to beat. Even if you don't think you have the "talent" to play music, its worth giving a shot for this reason alone.
